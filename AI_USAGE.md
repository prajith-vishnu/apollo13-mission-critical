# AI Usage Disclosure

This project was conceived, designed, and directed by **Prajith Vishnu Rajesh Kumar**. The defining creative decisions — the Mission Control flight-director perspective, the four simultaneous resource systems, the commitment to real NASA data throughout, the 1970s CRT console aesthetic, the post-commit historical-record reveal, and the overall game design and educational goals — originated from my vision for what this game should be.

**Claude (Anthropic) was used as a development assistant** to implement that vision: writing the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and drafting in-game text and documentation to my specifications. My role throughout was creative director and product owner — I specified what to build and why, set the research direction toward primary NASA sources, reviewed the output, play-tested the results, and directed every revision, including a full overhaul of the decision interface based on external playtest feedback. Development was iterative: specify, review, redirect, repeat.

This disclosure is consistent with Hack Club Stardance's AI usage policy, which permits AI assistance where participants demonstrate significant individual contribution and the core ideas are their own. The commit history of this repository reflects the AI assistance transparently (commits are co-authored with Claude).

All game concepts, the NASA research direction, the aesthetic choices, and the educational goals belong to the project creator.
